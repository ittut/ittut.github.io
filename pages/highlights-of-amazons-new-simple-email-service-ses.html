Highlights of Amazon’s New Simple Email Service (SES)

27th June 2012    

The five adjectives that can be used to describe Amazon’s new Simple Email Service (SES) are: simple, reliable, affordable, compatible, and scalable. While this email service is new, it promises to put positive aspects back into email marketing.

For too long now, businesses have had to deal with high costs, unreliability, and complicated services that just did not deliver what they promised. Amazon intends to change all of that with its latest creation of its Simple Email Service. SES is designed to simplify email marketing for businesses while bringing down the cost to a more affordable level.

Amazon has designed this email service to feature simplicity as part of the package. With SES, customers never need to worry about licensing issues. Since Amazon provides the infrastructure needed to host comprehensive email services, businesses can avoid the cost and time needed to build their own complex system.

Reliability is expected to be a given with SES since it runs with proven Amazon technology. Existing network infrastructure and Amazon data centers are well-known for their reliability, so it only follows that SES will benefit with the same high level of steadfast, reliable service.

SES is designed to be affordable for most businesses. Since no up-front fees exist, the savings a business can realize by signing on with Amazon’s SES begin immediately. Outside of the free usage tier, the cost to email is low and the fees for data transfer are just as reasonable.

Compatibility is expected since Amazon’s New Simple Email Service has been designed for implementation with Amazon’s other Web services. If you use one of Amazon’s other AWS services (Elastic Beanstalk or EC2), you can easily access SES from them. All you need is to incorporate one of Amazon’s AWS software kits for development.

Amazon’s SES utilizes some of the best scalable technology within its own websites. This scalability is featured with Amazon’s Simple Email Service.

Intro: Amazon’s new Simple Email Service promises businesses a fantastic opportunity to save money on their email-marketing services. Plus, it promises better service than third-party providers. Does this service have what it takes to provide great service without the added frustration of high cost?
